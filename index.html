FAQ

What does KBLock do?

KBLock is a macOS menu-bar utility that fixes your keyboard input source. If you switch between Chinese and English while typing, KBLock keeps the correct source on for each app — no more typing Chinese in terminals.

Is it free?

Yes, KBLock is free and open source under the MIT license.

Does KBLock send data anywhere?

No. All data stays on your device. iCloud sync uses your private iCloud account — we never see your data.

What permissions does it need?

Accessibility access (to switch your input source). That's it.

Requirements?

macOS 14 Sonoma or later. Apple Silicon and Intel both supported.

Privacy Policy

KBLock Privacy Policy

KBLock does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data. All application data (rules, settings, usage statistics) is stored locally on your device. If you enable iCloud Sync, data is synchronized through your personal iCloud account using NSUbiquitousKeyValueStore — no external servers are involved. The application does not contain any analytics SDK, tracking code, or telemetry. Accessibility access is used solely for switching keyboard input sources on your local machine.
